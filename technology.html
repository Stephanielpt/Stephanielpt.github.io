Computer  Model  of  a  Six-star  Orbiting System

  • Technology

    July 16, 2016

  • This summer I created a computer model of a six-star orbiting system.

    Not going to lie, it was incredibly intimidating at first, but it proved to be just as fun as it was challenging. I had to use the Newton's Laws of Gravitation to dictate how stars reacted to the others stars nearby. On top of that, I had to use 3D trigonometry to ensure the stars all started in an orientation that would allow them to maintain orbit around each other. We wouldn't want all of them to be attracted to an abnormally massive star and collide!
